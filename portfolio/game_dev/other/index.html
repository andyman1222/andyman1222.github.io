This is a collection of other miscellaneous games or projects in Unity, Unreal Engine, or another game engine. They are not complete, in fact some of it was just messing around in the engine. These are for games made in an engine only; I have some Java/C++ games programmed with basic 2D graphics or ASCII, viewable on my github page.


Horror game



This horror game started out not as a horror game, but rather a Unity js game made for the original version of this very website. The objective was to collect coins around the map and avoid cubes that gradually got closer and sped up towards you. From within the webite, the coins collected would be tracked.

Recently, I was messing around with the original project to see if it still works. I decided to mess around with the map's lighting, as well as add some lights on the cubes and coins, and noticed the game had a very different feel to it- the floating and approaching cubes seemed scary as a looming threat, especially with some omnious red lighting in a dark forest.

With that, I went on and further improved the setting within Unity, reworked the cube's movement a bit, and made a way for coins to respawn. I used lighting and colors to guide the player to explore the map, with green being the collectibles the player needs to collect, and red being the enemy the player needs to avoid. I think the result creates a fundamental horror video game, where the horror isn't always a jumpscare, or gore, but rather a looming threat that will eventually creep up behind or in front of you.


Get Over It In VR



"Get Over It In VR" is an experimental VR game I was testing to make in Unreal Engine 4. Based off the popular game "Getting Over It," "Get Over It In VR" puts the player in a pot and requires them to navigate by pushing a hammer against the ground.

The game is only a concept, as I'm not entirely sure how to accomplish the physics of the hammer to realisticly move the player around (for example, to cling on a cliffside). However, I may change the game in the future to experiment with other gameplay mechanics only capable in VR.

However, due to moving away to college, I have been unable to work effectively on the game, or anything similar in VR.


"Maze Game"




"Maze Game" was my first Unreal Engine project, made over 8 years ago, before Unreal Engine 4 was even made free to use. The game was the inspiration for Amazeing. The simple concept of this game was to solve a maze by collecting ammunition, which can be shot at to unblock obstacles (Amazeing uses this same concept and builds on it). The game was made in the course of a week-long summer camp about UE4. You can download the original game's source on Github.


Airplane dodging game



This airplane dodging game was a prototype I made while messing around with Unity's airplane starter pack. The game consists of a large, flat indoor area with trees scattered throughout, and a ceiling preventing the player from flying out. Ideally, what the player must do is fly as long and as fast as possible in an airplane in this space. The trees stood as obstacles which could slow, change the direction of, or even stop the player. However, there is no game end or finish programmed in, and there's a single UI element that doesn't even work properly.

Flight simulation (not working prototype)


Multi-plane flight game

I have made two different little prototypes for flight simulation. The first and more recent one, I have attempted to create a flight simulator using Unreal Engine's physics system. However, there are some forces or aspects of flight which I did not account for in UE4, and there's some bugginess with how I implemented the wheels, so the makeshift airplane does not yet fly. In the future I might go back and continue work on this.

The second flight prototype was something made in Unity. Using the starter pack flight scripts from Unity (the same ones used in "Airplane dodging game"), I created 5 player-controlled jets, and a bunch of AI-controlled jets, in sort of a dogfight. The interesting thing about this is that the player controls 5 independent aircraft, which they can use to fly in formations, or mess up in doing so. The AI aircraft also follow the main jet, adding some challenge to keep formation from crashing. The player can also switch views between their controllable aircraft with the number keys.


TimeStryke



TimeStryke was a project originally led by me some four years ago with my friends. We wanted to make a simple and interesting game, so we went with a 2D MMO similar to "Realm Of the Mad God." The game's prominent feature was that the maps would progress thru time- before, during, and after modern day. Multiple assets, code, and a complex game design document, including plans for PvP and even a story, were made, and in addition to management, I worked on making a server. However, over time we parted ways and the project is extremely unfinished.

As a group was working on this game, I do not take credit for the art made in the game. Furthermore, the game is currently in an unplayable state, so as of now I am unable to provide a gameplay demonstration.
